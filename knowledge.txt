The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system, off the coast of Queensland, Australia. It stretches over 2,300 kilometers and hosts thousands of species of fish and coral.

Mount Everest is the highest mountain on Earth at 8,849 meters. It sits on the border of Nepal and Tibet, first summited by Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953.

The Amazon rainforest produces about 20 percent of the world's oxygen and spans nine South American countries, mostly Brazil.

Honey never spoils. Archaeologists found edible honey in Egyptian tombs over 3,000 years old, thanks to its low moisture and acidity.
